The two charts illustrate the main trends in Facebook usage between 2012 and 2013 and provide incentives for using it between male and female.
As can be seen from the first chart, the desktop use of Facebook slightly fell from 2012 to 2013. On the contrary, Facebook mobile usage saw a sharp rise, though still remained considerbaly smaller than the desktop use. In the second chart it can be seen that the most common reason for using Facebook is sharing photos and videos and there is 15% more women than men that have chosen this reason. However, there is an equal percentage between male and female for the choice of the reason of receiveing updates from Facebook. Speaking about two other reasons, as seeing funny posts and learning about ways to help other, the percentage of women which chose these options slightly prevail the percantage of men.
Overall, it can be seen from the two charts that the mobile usage of Facebook increases as desktop use decreases. It can happen due to the fact that all the incentives that are chosen by people in the second chart, such as sharing photos and videos or receiving updated, can be done easily from the mobile phones. More than that, the chart shows that women tend to use social network more often than men.
